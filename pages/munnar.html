Munnar — Tea Gardens

Munnar in Kerala is famed for rolling tea plantations, misty hills, and a tranquil climate — ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

How to reach

  • By Air: Nearest airports: Cochin International Airport (COK) ~ 4–5 hours by road.
  • By Train: Nearest major railheads: Aluva / Ernakulam; then a scenic drive to Munnar.
  • By Road: Well-connected by state highways; many tourists hire a car from Kochi or take bus services.
  • Local travel: Taxis and rental bikes are common; roads are hilly — drive carefully.

Best spots & tips

  1. Tea Plantations: Walk through estates like Kolukkumalai and Anamudi viewpoints.
  2. Eravikulam National Park: Home to the Nilgiri Tahr and scenic hikes (permit & timings apply).
  3. Mattupetty Dam & Echo Point: Popular photo stops and boating options.

Recommended restaurants

  • Saravana Bhavan — Reliable South Indian vegetarian fare.
  • Rapsy Restaurant — Local favorites and homemade-style Kerala dishes.
  • Local tea cafés — Try freshly brewed estate teas and snacks at roadside stalls.

Recommended hotels & homestays

  • Luxury: Tea estate bungalows and boutique resorts (search: Booking.com Munnar).
  • Mid-range & Homestays: Many options offering local breakfast and guided walks.
  • Budget: Guesthouses near town center and bus stand.

Map

Essential info

Best time to visit: Sep–May. Mornings can be chilly — carry a light jacket. Respect private tea estates' rules and take permission for estate walks if required.
