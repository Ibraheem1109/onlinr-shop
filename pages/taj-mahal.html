Taj Mahal — Agra

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the new Seven Wonders of the World, is a white-marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

How to reach

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Agra Airport (AGC). Better options are New Delhi's Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL), then a 3–4 hour drive or train to Agra.
  • By Train: Agra is well-connected by rail. Fast trains from New Delhi: Gatimaan Express (about 1.5–2 hours) and other express trains.
  • By Road: NH 44 and Yamuna Expressway connect Delhi–Agra (approx 3–4 hours by car/bus).
  • Local travel: Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and cycle-rickshaws are available; use a local taxi or ride-hailing app for convenience.

Best spots & tips

  1. Sunrise & Sunset: Visit early morning for fewer crowds and soft light; sunset views from Mehtab Bagh are spectacular.
  2. Inside the mausoleum: Dress modestly; note limited camera use and security checks.
  3. Nearby: Agra Fort (a UNESCO site), Itmad-ud-Daulah (Baby Taj), and Mehtab Bagh.

Recommended restaurants

  • Pinch of Spice — Popular for North Indian meals; Tajganj area.
  • Dasaprakash — Well-known South Indian vegetarian spot.
  • Esphahan (The Oberoi) — Fine dining with classical ambiance (best for special occasions).

Essential info

Entry tickets, photography rules, and opening times change seasonally — check the official ASI (Archaeological Survey of India) site or local tourist counters before visiting. Carry water, sunscreen, and ID for entry.
