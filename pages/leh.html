Leh & Ladakh — High Altitude Adventures

Leh and Ladakh offer stark mountain landscapes, azure lakes like Pangong and Tso Moriri, high passes, and Buddhist monasteries.

How to reach

  • By Air: Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport (IXL) at Leh has flights from Delhi and other cities (subject to weather).
  • By Road: Road access via Manali–Leh Highway (summer only) or Srinagar–Leh Highway. These are long but scenic drives; travel only in suitable weather.
  • Permits: Foreign nationals need Inner Line Permits (ILP) for many areas; Indian tourists may require permits for certain routes — check current rules.
  • Local travel: Hire 4x4 taxis or motorbikes; acclimatize before undertaking high-altitude excursions.

Best spots & tips

  1. Pangong Tso: Famous high-altitude lake — best visited after acclimatizing; nighttime temperatures drop sharply.
  2. Thiksey & Hemis Monasteries: Excellent for culture and photography.
  3. High passes: Khardung La and Chang La offer epic views but check road conditions and acclimatization advice.
  4. Health: Acclimatize 24–48 hours in Leh before strenuous activity; carry altitude medication if advised.

Recommended restaurants

  • Tibetan Kitchen — Simple Tibetan & Ladakhi fare in Leh.
  • Bon Appetit — Popular with travelers for continental and Indian meals.
  • Open Sky Café — Good ambiance and local snacks.

Recommended stays

  • Luxury/Heritage: Boutique guesthouses and heritage hotels in Leh — search: Booking.com Leh
  • Camping: Pangong and Tso Moriri camps are available through local operators (seasonal).
  • Budget: Hostels and guesthouses in Leh town for backpackers.

Map

Essential info

Best time to visit: May–Sep (roads and passes open). Winters are extremely cold and many roads close. Respect fragile mountain ecology and local communities.
