Jaipur — The Pink City

Jaipur, Rajasthan's capital, is famous for its historic forts, palaces, colorful bazaars, and vibrant culture.

How to reach

  • By Air: Jaipur International Airport (JAI) has regular flights from major Indian cities.
  • By Train: Well-connected via rail to Delhi, Agra, and other cities (Jaipur Junction).
  • By Road: NH48 connects Jaipur with Delhi (~5 hours by car). Frequent state-run and private buses available.
  • Local travel: Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and ride-hailing apps; consider hiring a driver for day trips to Amber Fort and surrounding sites.

Best spots & tips

  1. Amber Fort: Arrive early to avoid crowds; elephant rides and jeep options available at the base.
  2. Hawa Mahal: Best photographed at sunrise from the street or from nearby rooftop cafés.
  3. City Palace & Jantar Mantar: Allow 2–3 hours to explore both.
  4. Markets: Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar for textiles, jewelry, and handicrafts — bargain respectfully.

Recommended restaurants

  • Chokhi Dhani — Rajasthani cultural village with traditional food and performances.
  • Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB) — Famous for Rajasthani sweets and thali.
  • Suvarna Mahal (Taj Rambagh Palace) — Fine dining with royal ambiance.

Essential info

Peak season: Oct–Mar. Carry sun protection during summer and water. Respect dress codes when visiting palaces and temples. Use licensed guides for a richer experience.
